# Information Gathering

## Methods Used
To understand the problem and figure out what the platform needs, two main methods were used: interviews with students and a short survey. The goal was to find out how often students lose items, what they currently do about it, and what they would want from a digital solution.

## Key Findings

### Current Behavior
Most students first check where they last were, then ask friends, then post on WhatsApp or Facebook groups. Very few students go to the campus security office or lost and found box. Many students said they did not even know there was a physical lost and found on campus.

### Pain Points Identified
- No central place to search for lost or found items
- Posts on social media groups get ignored or buried
- The physical lost and found office has limited hours and is inconvenient to visit
- Students who find items have no easy way to reach the owner

### What Students Want
- A simple board they can browse without needing to scroll through a chat group
- Ability to filter by item type or location on campus
- A way to contact the poster without sharing their phone number publicly
- Notifications when a new item matching their description is posted
