# Surveys

A short survey was shared with 20 students across different faculties to collect broader data on the lost and found problem.

---

## Survey Questions and Results

**Q1: Have you ever lost an item on campus?**
- Yes: 17 out of 20 (85%)
- No: 3 out of 20 (15%)

**Q2: What did you do when you lost the item?**
- Posted in a WhatsApp or social media group: 12
- Retraced steps and asked friends: 10
- Visited the campus lost and found office: 2
- Did nothing: 3
(Multiple answers allowed)

**Q3: Did you recover the item?**
- Yes: 6 out of 17 (35%)
- No: 11 out of 17 (65%)

**Q4: Have you ever found an item on campus and not known how to return it?**
- Yes: 9 out of 20 (45%)
- No: 11 out of 20 (55%)

**Q5: Would you use a dedicated campus lost and found website?**
- Yes: 16 out of 20 (80%)
- Maybe: 3 out of 20 (15%)
- No: 1 out of 20 (5%)

**Q6: Which features would you want? (Select all that apply)**
- Photo upload: 18
- Search by item type: 15
- Filter by campus location: 14
- Contact the poster without sharing phone number: 16
- Mark item as found/resolved: 12

---

## Key Takeaways
Most students have lost something on campus and most did not recover it. The main reason is there is no central place to search. 80% said they would use a dedicated platform. The most requested features are photo upload, private contact, and location filter. These findings directly shaped the features planned for this project.
