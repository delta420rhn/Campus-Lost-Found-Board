# Interviews

Five students were interviewed to understand their experience with losing or finding items on campus.

---

## Interview 1

**Participant:** 2nd year Computer Science student  
**Date:** June 2026

**Q: Have you ever lost something on campus?**  
Yes, I lost my student ID card last semester near the library. I posted in our class WhatsApp group but nobody replied. I ended up just replacing it.

**Q: Did you check the lost and found office?**  
Honestly no. I didn't know exactly where it was and I figured it wasn't worth the effort.

**Q: Would you use a campus lost and found website?**  
Definitely. Even just being able to search by item type would be way more useful than scrolling through a WhatsApp group.

---

## Interview 2

**Participant:** 3rd year Business student  
**Date:** June 2026

**Q: Have you ever found something on campus and tried to return it?**  
Yes, I found a phone in the cafeteria. I gave it to a staff member at the counter but I don't know what happened to it after that.

**Q: What would you have preferred to do?**  
If there was a website I could post a photo on, the owner could have seen it and come to me directly. That would have been faster.

---

## Interview 3

**Participant:** 1st year Engineering student  
**Date:** June 2026

**Q: What do you usually do when you lose something?**  
I retrace my steps and ask people nearby. I've never gone to any lost and found office.

**Q: What would make you use a lost and found app?**  
If it was simple. Like just a list of items with photos and a message button. Nothing complicated.

---

## Interview 4

**Participant:** 4th year student  
**Date:** June 2026

**Q: Do you think students would actually use a lost and found platform?**  
It depends on how easy it is. If everyone is already using it, then yes. It needs to be the go-to place from day one.

**Q: What feature would you find most useful?**  
Search by location on campus. If I lost my bag near the gym I want to filter by gym, not scroll through everything.
