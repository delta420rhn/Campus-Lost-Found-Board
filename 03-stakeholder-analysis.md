# Stakeholder Analysis

## 1. Students (Primary Users)
Students are the main users of the platform. They are both the ones who lose items and the ones who find and post items. Their main needs are a simple way to post an item, a way to search for their lost belongings, and a way to contact another student without sharing personal contact info publicly.

**Interest level:** High  
**Influence:** High  
**Needs:** Easy posting, search, contact system, mobile-friendly

## 2. University Administration
The university benefits from having a proper system to handle lost and found. It reduces pressure on security offices and shows the institution cares about student welfare. They may want to be aware of what the platform handles but are not directly involved in day-to-day usage.

**Interest level:** Medium  
**Influence:** Medium  
**Needs:** The system should not expose student data and should not replace official admin channels entirely

## 3. Campus Security / Student Affairs Office
These staff currently manage physical lost and found boxes. They would be indirectly affected because some students would use this platform instead of going to them. They could potentially become moderators or admins in a future version.

**Interest level:** Medium  
**Influence:** Low  
**Needs:** Awareness of the platform, possible integration later

## 4. Developer (Project Owner)
The developer is responsible for building, maintaining, and improving the platform. They need clear requirements and feedback from students to keep the system useful.

**Interest level:** High  
**Influence:** High  
**Needs:** Clear scope, user feedback, working infrastructure
